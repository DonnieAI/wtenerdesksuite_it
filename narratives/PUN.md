### PUN Index GME: The New Italian Electricity Market Index

Starting in **2025**, Italy replaced the traditional **Prezzo Unico Nazionale (PUN)** with the **PUN Index GME** as the reference wholesale electricity price.

The new index is calculated by **Gestore dei Mercati Energetici (GME)** and reflects the **weighted average price of electricity traded in the Day-Ahead Market (MGP)**, taking into account zonal price differences across Italy.

#### Why It Was Introduced

The reform aligns Italy with the **European Price Coupling of Regions (PCR)** initiative, which aims to create a harmonized EU day-ahead electricity market using a single algorithm called **Euphemia**.

This algorithm:

- Optimizes cross-border energy allocation
- Harmonizes price formation across Europe
- Maximizes social welfare
- Improves price transparency

####  How It Is Calculated

Unlike the old PUN, which applied a single national price, the **PUN Index GME** is based on:

- **Hourly zonal prices**, which differ by geographic area
- The **volume of electricity traded** in each zone
- A **weighted average** of these zonal prices

The index is calculated **hourly** and then aggregated into **daily and monthly values**.

#### Key Differences from the Old PUN

- **Old PUN**: One uniform national price
- **PUN Index GME**: Reflects zonal market dynamics and geographic price differentiation


####  Energy Generation

- Greater valuation of local renewable production
- Areas with abundant renewables may experience lower prices
- High-demand, low-production zones may face higher prices

####  Trading and Retail

- Increased price volatility
- More sophisticated hedging strategies required for energy retailers

####  Infrastructure

- Potential reduction in grid congestion
- Possible need for stronger transmission networks in structurally deficit areas

#### Impact on Consumers

- Greater price transparency
- Increased tariff differentiation between Northern, Central, and Southern Italy

#### Short Term

A transitional equalization mechanism established by **ARERA**  
(valid until **31 December 2025**, under Decree 210/2021)  
limits abrupt price impacts.

#### Long Term

- Electricity bills may vary more significantly by location
- The new system may encourage:
  - Energy efficiency
  - Demand shifting
  - Reduced peak-hour consumption
