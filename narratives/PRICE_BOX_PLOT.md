#### How to Read a Boxplot

This figure shows the distribution of monthly selected price variable for each year.
For every year:

- The **box** represents the middle 50% of monthly prices (the **Interquartile Range, IQR**).
- The **horizontal line inside the box** represents the median monthly price.
- The **whiskers** extend to **1.5 × IQR**, capturing most of the typical variation in prices.
- Extreme outliers beyond this range are not emphasized, allowing clearer comparison across years.

Interpretation:
- A **taller box** indicates greater price dispersion within that year.
- A **higher position of the box** indicates a higher overall price level.
This visualization allows comparison of:

- Changes in the price level across years
- Changes in intra-year price variability
